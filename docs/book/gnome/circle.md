# GNOME Circle

> The GNOME community's program for high-quality third-party apps. Acceptance means your app gets official recognition, an `org.gnome.*`-style placement, and a community of fellow maintainers.

## What this chapter covers

- What Circle is, and what it isn't (it's *not* "becoming GNOME Core").
- Benefits: visibility, hosted infrastructure, mentorship, sometimes funding.
- The requirements (read the current Circle wiki for the authoritative list — this chapter is a paraphrase):
    - Follows the HIG.
    - Uses the GNOME platform appropriately (libadwaita where it fits, portals, GSettings, GNotification).
    - Distributed on Flathub.
    - Adaptive layout (works on small screens).
    - Translatable, with infrastructure set up.
    - Accessible (keyboard, screen reader, contrast).
    - AppStream metainfo is complete and valid.
    - Branding is consistent, app icon follows the style.
    - Stable release history.
    - Open source under a GNOME-acceptable license.
- The application process: where to apply, what the reviewers look at, the feedback loop.
- Surviving review: a self-checklist that mirrors the reviewer checklist.
- After acceptance: what changes (placement, expectations), what doesn't (your codebase remains yours).
- When *not* to apply: app too young, maintenance not sustained, design doesn't aim at GNOME.

## What you'll be able to do

- Decide if Circle is a goal for your app.
- Self-assess against the program's bar.
- Apply with a high chance of acceptance.
