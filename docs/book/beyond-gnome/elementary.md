# elementary OS

> elementary uses GTK and has its own design language (Pantheon) and toolkit additions (Granite). Your goi app can integrate respectfully.

## What this chapter covers

- The relationship: elementary is downstream-of-GTK, with their own HIG, theme, and patterns.
- Granite: elementary's libadwaita analogue. What it provides (widgets, helpers) and how it diverges.
- Following elementary HIG when on elementary: headerbar style, typography, button shapes.
- AppCenter: elementary's curated app store, separate from Flathub (though they ship Flathub too).
- Distribution paths: AppCenter Flatpak listing, the curated pay-what-you-want model.
- Where elementary apps diverge from GNOME apps: different naming, different developer tooling, different community.
- Runtime detection: deciding whether to apply elementary-specific tweaks at startup.

## What you'll be able to do

- Make a goi app feel at home on elementary.
- Decide whether to dual-publish on AppCenter and Flathub.

## Notes for the writer

- Short chapter; elementary's audience is smaller but devoted.
- Be respectful — elementary developers have strong opinions on app polish that are worth heeding.
