# Getting started

> What goi is, what it's for, and how it compares to the alternatives — so you can decide whether to keep reading.

## What this chapter covers

- What goi is: a Python binding to GObject-based libraries (GTK, GLib, Adwaita, …) generated from GObject Introspection metadata.
- How it differs from PyGObject (the older, widely-used binding) and why both exist.
- What you can build with it: desktop apps for Linux, Windows, and macOS; GNOME-platform apps in particular.
- The shape of a goi app: a `Gtk.Application`, some widgets, a main loop.
- Where this book takes you and how to navigate it.

## What you'll be able to do

- Decide whether goi is the right tool for your project.
- Understand the words "GObject," "introspection," and "typelib" well enough to follow the rest of the book.
